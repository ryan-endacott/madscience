# Literature Review: Pilot Whale Mass Strandings
*Compiled March 2026 from web searches of published research*

## 1. The Magnetic Hypothesis

### Kirschvink (1986) — Original Evidence
- Analyzed 212 live stranding events in the US
- Found **significant tendency for strandings near coastal locations with local magnetic minima**
- Monte Carlo simulations confirmed significance
- Proposed cetaceans use magnetic topography for navigation
- Source: [J. Exp. Biol. 120:1-24](https://journals.biologists.com/jeb/article/120/1/1/4953/Evidence-from-Strandings-for-Geomagnetic)

### Klinowska (1985) — UK Data
- First to propose magnetic navigation → stranding link
- Found association between stranding positions and areas where magnetic minima intersect the coast
- Reasoning: healthy whales that strand alive must have made a navigational mistake

### Kirschvink (1992) — Fin Whale Migration
- Fin whale sighting positions associated with areas of low geomagnetic intensity and gradient
- Suggests magnetic contours serve as migration "highways"
- Source: [J. Exp. Biol. 171:67-78](https://web.gps.caltech.edu/~jkirschvink/pdfs/FinWhaleNavigation.pdf)

### Dolphin Magnetoreception — Experimental Evidence
- Behavioral experiments showed dolphins can detect experimental magnetic fields
- Source: [Kremers et al. 2014, Naturwissenschaften](https://hal.science/hal-01134557/document)

### Solar Storms (Granger et al. 2020)
- Gray whales 4.3x more likely to strand during high solar radio-frequency noise
- Mechanism: RF noise overwhelms magnetic sense, not direct field distortion
- Source: [Current Biology, Feb 2020](https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2020/02/200224111356.htm)

### Geomagnetic Storms and Mass Strandings — Negative
- EOS analysis: geomagnetic storms probably don't cause **mass** cetacean strandings
- Seasonal patterns overlap coincidentally
- Source: [EOS Research Spotlight](https://eos.org/research-spotlights/geomagnetic-storms-probably-dont-cause-mass-cetacean-strandings)

### Our Analysis (March 2026) — Definitive Null
- IGRF-14 gradients: t=-0.007 (15 sites, 8 hotspots, 7 controls)
- EMAG2v3 crustal anomalies: t=0.075 (same sites)
- Isoline geometry: t=-0.659
- **Note**: We tested whether gradients DIFFER between hotspots and controls. Kirschvink tested whether strandings occur at magnetic MINIMA on the coastline. These are different claims.

## 2. Social Cohesion / Follow-the-Leader

- When one whale strands, pod follows due to strong social bonds
- Widely considered a **necessary amplifier** but not a root cause
- Genetic studies show stranded groups aren't always single families → complicates pure kinship explanation
- Source: [NSF-funded study](https://par.nsf.gov/servlets/purl/10292135)
- Explains WHY mass strandings happen (vs individual) but not WHERE or WHEN

## 3. Oceanographic Conditions / Prey Chasing

### Zellar et al. (2021) — Cape Cod
- 18 years of stranding reports + chlorophyll, wind, tide, ocean current data
- Wind speeds consistently increased 1 month before stranding events
- Hypothesis: winds and currents push prey (squid) inshore, whales follow
- Cape Cod averaged 11 mass stranding events per year (1999-2017)
- Source: [Geophysical Research Letters](https://agupubs.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1029/2021GL093697)

### Pilot Whale Diet
- Northwestern Atlantic: primarily short-finned squid
- Also: octopus, mackerel, herring, cod, turbot, hake, dogfish
- Follow prey into continental-shelf waters
- Source: [NOAA Fisheries](https://www.fisheries.noaa.gov/species/long-finned-pilot-whale/science)

## 4. Coastal Geometry / Echolocation Failure

### Farewell Spit Trap
- Hook-shaped spit, 80km² tidal mud flats exposed at low tide
- Gently sloping sandy beaches reflect echolocation clicks away
- Whales "see" open water ahead when approaching the beach
- NZ DOC explicitly calls it a "whale trap"
- Source: [NZ DOC](https://www.doc.govt.nz/nature/native-animals/marine-mammals/marine-mammal-strandings/why-do-marine-mammals-strand/)

### General Pattern
- Stranding hotspots share: gently sloping, sandy beaches
- **But**: Our bathymetry analysis shows Dutch Wadden Sea has even gentler slopes and MORE shallow water but zero strandings → geometry alone isn't sufficient

## 5. Navy Sonar (Strong for Beaked Whales, Weak for Pilot Whales)

- Conclusive link to **beaked whale** strandings (Bahamas 2000, Canary Islands, Mariana Islands)
- Cuvier's beaked whales found with hemorrhages near ears after sonar exercises
- 12 "atypical" mass strandings statistically correlated with naval exercises
- **But**: Pilot whale mass strandings predate sonar by centuries → sonar may contribute but isn't the primary driver
- Sources: [Frontiers in Marine Science](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/marine-science/articles/10.3389/fmars.2017.00295/full), [Royal Society](https://royalsocietypublishing.org/doi/10.1098/rspb.2020.0070)

## 6. Disease

- 2025 study: 62% of cetacean strandings involved disease (morbillivirus, brucella)
- More relevant to individual strandings than mass events
- POPs (persistent organic pollutants) found in mass-stranded pilot whales in Iceland
- Source: [University of Hawaii 2025](https://www.hawaii.edu/news/2025/12/21/whale-dolphin-strandings/), [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0025326X23011931)

## 7. Open Questions / What's Testable

### Untested with existing data:
1. **Oceanographic conditions at ALL 15 sites** (Zellar only studied Cape Cod)
   - Wind, current, chlorophyll data available from NOAA/NASA
2. **Shipping lane proximity** — noise pollution, physical displacement
   - AIS vessel traffic data is publicly available
3. **Stranding timing vs prey migration** — do strandings cluster with squid seasons?
4. **Kirschvink's specific claim** — magnetic minima at exact stranding coordinates (we tested gradient differences between sites, not minima at strand points)
5. **Interaction effects** — maybe it's shallow geometry + prey conditions + social cohesion together, not any single factor

### Requires new data:
- Sub-km aeromagnetic surveys at specific sites
- Acoustic monitoring at stranding sites
- Whale tracking data during stranding events
