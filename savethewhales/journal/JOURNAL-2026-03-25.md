## EMAG2v3 Crustal Anomaly Analysis

Downloaded the full EMAG2v3 dataset (Meyer et al. 2017, DOI: 10.7289/V5H70CVX) — 1.1GB CSV with 58.3M data points at 2-arc-minute (~3.7km) resolution. Extracted ±1° regions around all 15 sites (51,007 points total).

**Result**: No significant difference between hotspots and controls on any metric.

| Metric | Hotspot mean | Control mean | t-stat |
|--------|-------------|-------------|--------|
| Anomaly variability (σ) | 48.9 nT | 47.0 nT | 0.075 |
| Anomaly range | 241.5 nT | 216.4 nT | 0.223 |
| Max gradient | 11.2 nT/km | 10.2 nT/km | 0.166 |
| Gradient-coast angle | 48.5° | 48.9° | -0.027 |

The Norwegian Coast control site is a massive outlier (σ=184 nT) which inflates control group variance. But even excluding it, no metric approaches significance.

## ETOPO Bathymetry Analysis

Downloaded ETOPO1 bathymetry (1 arc-minute) for all 15 sites via NOAA ERDDAP API. Tested whether coastal geometry predicts strandings.

**Result**: No significant difference.

| Metric | Hotspot mean | Control mean | t-stat |
|--------|-------------|-------------|--------|
| Shallow/Ocean ratio | 36.4% | 60.3% | -1.340 |
| Shallow zone slope | 5.5 m/km | 7.1 m/km | -0.400 |
| Ocean fraction | 70.6% | 57.5% | 1.121 |

**Key counterexample**: Dutch Wadden Sea has 98% shallow water and 0.7 m/km slope (the "perfect" whale trap by geometry) but zero mass strandings. Simple bathymetry does not predict stranding sites.

## Literature Review

Compiled a comprehensive review of whale stranding science. See `LITERATURE-REVIEW-2026-03.md` for full details. Key findings:

- **Kirschvink (1986)**: Found strandings correlate with magnetic *minima* at specific coastline points. Different from our test (gradient differences between sites).
- **Zellar et al. (2021)**: Wind speeds increase 1 month before Cape Cod strandings. Most promising lead.
- **Social cohesion**: Amplifier, not root cause. Stranded groups aren't always kin.
- **Sonar**: Strong for beaked whales, weak for pilot whales (strandings predate sonar).
- **Disease**: 62% of individual strandings, but less relevant for mass events.

## Pivot: Stranding Risk Early Warning System

Given all physical single-variable hypotheses are null, pivoted to building a practical tool.

### Prototype Risk Model (Farewell Spit)

Built `code/stranding_risk_model.py` combining seasonality, SST anomaly, and chlorophyll data from ERDDAP.

**Result: t=4.348 (SIGNIFICANT)**
- 91% of stranding months score above median risk
- Validated against 11 events (2003-2024)
- Primarily driven by seasonality (19/20 events in Nov-Mar)

## AutoScience Framework

Built an automated hypothesis testing framework at `autoscience/`:
- `prepare.py`: loads cached data, computes 74 features (lags, anomalies, deltas, multi-month averages)
- `hypothesis.py`: single file that gets modified each iteration
- `evaluate.py`: runs hypothesis, computes t-statistic, logs to `experiments.jsonl`
- `program.md`: research directives for the agent

Ran 15 experiments iterating different hypothesis formulations.

### Critical Data Inspection

Compared stranding months to *other summer months* (the fair comparison):

| Feature | Stranding mean | Other summer mean | t-stat |
|---------|---------------|------------------|--------|
| wind_speed_anom | +0.559 | -0.062 | **+2.19** |
| chlorophyll_avg3m | 0.867 | 1.054 | **-2.20** |
| sst_anom_lag1 | +0.391 | -0.049 | +1.56 |

**Key finding**: Only wind speed anomaly is truly significant within summer months. Stranding months are windier than usual.

**Surprising**: Low chlorophyll (not high) correlates with strandings. Hypothesis: when offshore productivity is low, prey concentrates closer to shore, drawing whales into the trap.

### Honest Assessment

The high t-statistics (up to 8.09) are primarily driven by the summer/non-summer binary split. Within summer months, only wind speed anomaly adds significant signal. The environmental variables improve the model but seasonality is doing the heavy lifting.
